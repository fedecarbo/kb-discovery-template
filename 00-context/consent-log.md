## Why this exists

As the project scales (readouts to stakeholders, sharing prototypes, writing up findings publicly), we need to know per-participant: did they agree to be recorded? To be named? To be re-contacted? To have quotes used in a public deliverable? Without a structured record, we'll either be over-cautious (losing rich material) or over-permissive (using something we shouldn't).

This is also a check on ourselves: at intake, every participant should be asked about each dimension explicitly, not assumed.

---

## Consent dimensions

| Dimension | Means |
|---|---|
| **Recording** | Audio, video, or transcript captured at all |
| **Internal use** | Material usable by our project team for analysis |
| **Named in internal docs** | Real name used in synthesis, insights, glossary |
| **Re-contact for follow-up** | We can come back to them with more questions |
| **Prototype testing** | They're willing to test future prototypes with us |
| **Quoted in readouts (anonymised)** | Quote may appear in stakeholder-facing or external readout with name removed |
| **Quoted in readouts (named)** | Quote may appear in stakeholder-facing or external readout attributed |
| **Public or published use** | Material may appear in publications, press, conference talks |

Defaults are conservative: if a dimension wasn't explicitly agreed, treat it as **no**.

## Intake checklist (use at every interview)

At the start of every session, capture explicit yes or no on each dimension. Suggested phrasing:

> "Before we start: a couple of quick consent questions. We'll be recording, is that OK? We may want to come back to you with follow-up questions, OK? We'd like to share what we learn in a readout. Would you be OK with us quoting you in that, with or without your name attached? And later on, would you be open to testing a prototype?"

Record the answers in this log immediately after the session.
