# Consent log

> [!NOTE]
> **What this file is.** A per-participant record of what each person agreed to. Captured at intake; updated if anything changes. The single source of truth for what we can and can't do with each session's material. Append-only by row — if consent changes, add a new dated row rather than editing the old.

- **Last updated:** *YYYY-MM-DD.*

---

## Why this exists

As the project scales — stakeholder readouts, prototype testing, public write-ups — we need to know per participant: did they agree to be recorded? To be named? To be re-contacted? To have quotes used in a public deliverable?

Without a structured record, we'll either be over-cautious (losing rich material) or over-permissive (using something we shouldn't).

This file is also a check on ourselves. At intake, every participant should be asked about each dimension explicitly, not assumed.

---

## Consent dimensions

| Dimension | Means |
| --- | --- |
| **Recording** | Audio, video, or transcript captured at all. |
| **Internal use** | Material usable by our project team for analysis. |
| **Named in internal docs** | Real name used in synthesis, insights, glossary. |
| **Re-contact for follow-up** | We can come back with more questions. |
| **Prototype testing** | Willing to test future prototypes with us. |
| **Quoted in readouts (anonymised)** | Quote may appear in stakeholder-facing or external readout with name removed. |
| **Quoted in readouts (named)** | Quote may appear in stakeholder-facing or external readout attributed. |
| **Public or published use** | Material may appear in publications, press, conference talks. |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Defaults are conservative.** If a dimension wasn't explicitly agreed, treat it as **no**.

## Intake checklist

> [!TIP]
> Use at every interview. Capture an explicit yes or no on each dimension.

Suggested phrasing:

> "Before we start, a couple of quick consent questions. We'll be recording — is that OK? We may want to come back to you with follow-up questions — OK? We'd like to share what we learn in a readout. Would you be OK with us quoting you, with or without your name attached? And later on, would you be open to testing a prototype?"

Record the answers in this log immediately after the session.
