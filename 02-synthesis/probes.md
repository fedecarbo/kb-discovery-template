## The point

Insights accumulate support easily. They only get *strong* if we actively try to break them and fail. This file makes that work first-class: for every insight, we explicitly write down "here's what would contradict it; here's who could tell us." If we can't articulate either, the insight isn't ready to graduate from `hunch` to `emerging`.

---

## Promotion rule

An insight's status (`hunch` → `emerging` → `strong` → `contested`) reflects evidence weight **and** disconfirmation attempts:

- **hunch:** 1 supporting source. No disconfirmation attempted yet.
- **emerging:** 2–3 supporting sources, AND at least 1 session that *could* have contradicted it but didn't. The probe must have been live going into the session.
- **strong:** 4+ supporting sources, AND at least 1 deliberate disconfirmation attempt (interviewing a participant picked for being likely to contradict) that failed to find contradiction.
- **contested:** at least 1 session that materially contradicts the claim. Don't rewrite; flag and either narrow the claim or split it.

## What this list isn't

Not a research plan. The plan is the next interview. This list is the *constraint* on that plan: don't run another session with the same slice of participant unless we're deliberately stacking evidence; otherwise pick the probe with the highest priority and recruit accordingly.
